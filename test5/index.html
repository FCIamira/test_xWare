Study: The moon contains more water than we expected

While the moon was considered dry at one time, a new study shows that the moon contains more water than we expected, and this would turn the dream of life on the surface of the moon into a tangible reality. What is known about water on the moon so far: 2008: Researchers monitor lunar rocks for traces of trace amounts of water. 2009: Impacts from a crater crash site on the lunar south pole produce traces associated with water ice and hydroxyl. 2010: A closer look at collected moon rocks shows more traces of water in a mineral called apatite. 2017: First attempt to find the amount of water inside the moon by using satellite maps of volcanic debris.
